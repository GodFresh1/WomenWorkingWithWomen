How we started

Belinda Bethea Smith, CDT, CFDMI

Evangelist, Facilitator, Presenter, Keynote, Speaker


Belinda Smith is known as a trail blazer in her own right. For 23 years she enjoyed her career in Dialysis {end stage renal disease}. She began her internship as a technician and later became an active advocate for all dialysis technicians nationally. As a supervisory dialysis trainer she was then appointed to the Board of Directors of the National Association of Nephrology Technicians/Technology. After her appointment to the Board she was voted to be the first Black female president in its presidency history. While in that position, she fostered a partnership with the American Nephrology Nursing Association {ANNA}, where they collaborated and developed a training DVD to be used by all dialysis technicians.

Belinda later went on to be a dialysis clinical specialist for many laboratories across the country, where she provided educational in-services, seminars, and workshops. After working as a clinical specialist for many years Belinda decided moved to Gainesville, Florida to follow her passion as a seminar presenter. God continued to lead her on her professional journey as she became involved in the women’s ministry for the Southeastern Conference. It was here that she was offered an opportunity to follow this new ministry by providing workshops and seminars that covered SEC District One’s areas of Georgia and Florida. This included 22 churches and where her ministry flourished for 11 years.

Her objective is to empower women, teens, and families by providing the tools needed to establish and maintain healthy families and strong relationships.

For the past three and a half years, God has allowed her to pursue entrepreneurial work which fulfills her passions. God has also met every need in forming the organization “Women Working With Women, Inc.”

Mission

In order to enhance women's lives, we help provide tools that will drive their professional growth.

Values

Our mission is to recover our community through educating, enhancing, empowering, strengthening, and improving women and families personally, professionally, socially, and physically. We are passionate about helping women recognize their need and potential to restore their family through thoughtful study, educational workshops, seminars, and conferences.
